Security

How we protect the camera, the account, and the call.

Last updated: April 26, 2026

GoShowMe is built around a strict no-recording posture and modern WebRTC primitives. This page is the honest, unsweetened version of where we are today and what we are working on next.

Architecture

GoShowMe is a peer-to-peer WebRTC application with a thin signaling and session-management backend. The customer's phone establishes a direct video and audio connection to the tech's browser. The signaling channel coordinates the connection but never carries the media.

Current posture

Live Encrypted media in transit WebRTC mandates DTLS-SRTP for video and audio. Streams are encrypted between the two browsers.
Live HTTPS-only signaling All HTTP and WebSocket traffic to our backend is TLS-encrypted.
Live No video recording We do not record, persist, or post-process the camera stream.
Live Peer-to-peer first Streams travel direct between browsers when a route is available. TURN relay is used only when NAT traversal fails.
Live One-time session links Each session has a unique token with an expiration window chosen by the tech.
Live Camera consent screen Customers see who is asking and must explicitly allow the camera in their browser.
Live Hashed passwords Account passwords are stored with a strong hash. We never see them in plain text.
Planned SSO sign-in Google and Microsoft SSO for tech accounts.
Planned SOC 2 Type II Formal third-party attestation. We are starting the readiness program.
Planned HIPAA / BAA Available on Team plans for healthcare buyers.

Vision AI and third parties

When you press a Vision AI button, a still frame and your prompt are forwarded to OpenAI through our backend. We do not retain the frame or the response. OpenAI's API data policy states that data submitted via the API is not used to train their models. If your environment cannot send camera frames to a third-party LLM, do not enable the Vision AI features.

Subprocessors

We use a small set of subprocessors strictly to operate the service:

  • Railway — application hosting and signaling.
  • Cloudflare or Metered — TURN relay for fallback connectivity.
  • OpenAI — Vision AI analysis (only when invoked).
  • Our payment processor — for paid plans (data limited to billing identifiers).

Account security

  • Password hashes use a modern, salted algorithm.
  • Sessions are bound to a bearer token stored in browser local storage.
  • Sign-out invalidates the token on the server.
  • You can change your password at any time from the dashboard.

Data retention

  • Camera streams: not stored.
  • Chat messages: relayed in transit, not stored after the session ends.
  • Annotations and snapshots: stored locally on the tech's device, not on our servers, unless you explicitly export.
  • Session metadata (label, expiration, usage counter): kept on the tech account for usage tracking.
  • Account data: retained while the account is active; deleted within 60 days of account deletion.

Report a vulnerability

If you believe you have found a security issue, please send a description to security@goshowmeapp.com. Include reproduction steps where possible. We will acknowledge within 72 hours and work with you in good faith. Please do not publicly disclose the issue until we have a chance to investigate and remediate.

Bug bounty

We do not currently run a paid bounty program but we credit responsible reporters in our changelog if they wish to be named.

Status

For incidents and uptime updates, write to hi@goshowmeapp.com. A formal status page is on the roadmap.
